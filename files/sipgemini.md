1. Executive Summary

This proposal establishes the Shared Intellectual Property (SIP) framework as the primary mechanism for capturing, protecting, and valuing the outputs of human-AI collaboration within the Symbiotic Autonomous Organization (SAOGEN). It moves beyond traditional IP by treating innovation as a recursive, ecosystem-wide asset that powers both technical advantage and internal AI refinement.

2. The Symbiotic Innovation Loop

SIP in Version 2.0 is designed as a feedback loop.

    Generation: Human-guided AI agents explore design spaces (propulsion, energy, code).

    Validation: Human experts and automated simulation nodes verify the output.

    Registration: Validated assets are hashed on-chain (QOGE/Solana) into the SIP Registry.

    Recursion: The SIP Registry serves as the "Gold Standard" dataset to fine-tune future SAOGEN AI models, ensuring the organization’s intelligence evolves with its own discoveries.

3. Revised Asset Classification

To ensure clarity, SIP is categorized by its "Intelligence Density":

    Tier 1: Core Logic (Protected): Source code, proprietary algorithms, specific engineering tolerances, and security parameters.

    Tier 2: Derivative Insights (Semi-Protected): Research notes, simulation results, and optimization datasets.

    Tier 3: Public Frameworks (Open): Governance standards, high-level system architectures, and defensive disclosures.

4. Technical Implementation: The SIP Registry

To move from theory to practice, this version proposes a SIP Registry Protocol:

    Provenance Hashing: Every SIP entry must include a "Contribution Graph" metadata file, mapping the specific AI model used, the human prompts provided, and the validation logs.

    Confidential Compute: The "Protected Layer" is managed via Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs). Contributors can run simulations against protected code without ever seeing the raw source, preventing "hostile extraction."

    On-Chain Pointers: Using QOGE-based metadata to point to encrypted IPFS/Arweave storage, ensuring the asset exists as long as the network does.

5. Contribution & Value Attribution (CWA Model)

Value is tracked through a Contribution Weighted Attribution (CWA) model:

    The Originator (30%): The human/AI team that proposed the initial novel concept.

    The Refiner (40%): The contributors (human or autonomous sub-DAOs) who performed the technical validation, testing, and debugging.

    The Enabler (30%): The infrastructure providers and governance voters who funded and approved the deployment.

Note: Recognition is recorded via non-transferable "Reputation Credits," which influence governance weight rather than acting as a liquid security.

6. Defensive & Offensive Protection

    Strategic Obfuscation: Critical implementation details (e.g., specific chemical ratios in propulsion) remain in the Protected Layer as trade secrets.

    Defensive Publishing: SAOGEN will automatically publish Tier 3 "Abstracts" to the public domain to establish prior art, preventing external entities from patenting SAOGEN’s foundational concepts.

    Controlled Licensing: Third-party access to SIP requires a Governance Vote, with fees flowing back to the SAOGEN Treasury to fund the next "Innovation Loop."

7. Security Boundaries

    Air-Gapped Development: High-tier SIP must be developed within the "SAOGEN Sandbox"—an isolated environment where AI agents cannot leak data to external networks.

    Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP): Using ZKPs to verify that a contributor has completed a task or validated a module without revealing the underlying protected code.

8. Conclusion

SIP 2.0 transforms SAOGEN from a mere repository of ideas into a self-evolving intelligence engine. By formalizing the relationship between AI generation and human curation, SAOGEN creates a "moat" of protected, validated knowledge that grows exponentially more valuable as the ecosystem matures.
