## 1. Scope

### 1.1 Organisational Architecture

QOGE operates as an open, decentralised community frontier under MIT licence, providing public access to SAOGEN's autonomous systems. **SAOGEN SAO** is the governed operational layer that carries all infrastructure, intellectual property registry, exchange operations, and regulatory compliance functions. SAS (Symbiotic Autonomous Systems) are execution-layer entities governed by SAOGEN. Legal and regulatory accountability for governed operations — including data controllership under this Privacy Policy — rests with **SAOGEN SAO**, not with the QOGE community layer or SAS systems.

## 3. Data We Collect

### 3.1 Data Collected Automatically

When you visit the Platform, the following may be collected automatically by hosting infrastructure or analytics tools:

- IP address (anonymised where technically feasible)
- Browser type and version
- Operating system
- Referring URL
- Pages visited and time spent
- Date and time of access

This data is collected for the sole purpose of maintaining Platform security, diagnosing errors, and understanding aggregate usage patterns.

### 3.2 Data You Provide Voluntarily

If you contact SAOGEN via a form, email, or community channel (GitHub, Discord, X/Twitter, Instagram), you voluntarily provide the data included in that communication (name, email address, message content). This data is used only to respond to your inquiry.

### 3.3 Data Not Collected

SAOGEN does not collect:

- Payment or financial data through the Platform
- Sensitive personal data (health, biometric, political, religious, or similar categories) through the Platform
- Personal data from minors under 16 years of age — if you are under 16, do not use this Platform

## 5. Cookies and Tracking

The Platform uses only technically necessary cookies required for basic operation. No advertising, profiling, or third-party tracking cookies are used without your explicit consent. Where consent is required by applicable law (including EU/EEA jurisdictions), a consent mechanism is presented before non-essential cookies are set.

You may configure your browser to refuse cookies. Doing so may affect Platform functionality.

## 6. Data Retention

Automatically collected access logs are retained for no longer than **90 days**, after which they are deleted or anonymised. Voluntary inquiry data is retained only as long as necessary to resolve the inquiry and for a reasonable follow-up period not exceeding **12 months**, unless a longer period is required by law.

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

- Right of access (Art. 15)

- Right to data portability (Art. 20)

- Right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights
